My City - Patna

Patna is the capital city of Bihar, located on the southern bank of the Ganges River. The city was historically known as Pataliputra, the capital of the ancient Maurya and Gupta empires. One of Patna's most iconic landmarks is the Golghar, a large granary built in 1786 with a unique beehive shape . The Pathar Ki Masjid, constructed in 1621 by Parviz Mirza, is a notable Indo-Islamic architectural site in the city . Patna University, established in 1917, is one of the oldest universities in India . The city is a major educational hub, home to institutions like Patna Medical College and National Institute of Technology Patna. Patna Junction is a significant railway station, connecting the city to various parts of India. The Mahatma Gandhi Setu, one of the longest river bridges in India, connects Patna to Hajipur across the Ganges. Patna Sahib Gurudwara, the birthplace of Guru Gobind Singh, is a revered Sikh pilgrimage site. The city is known for its vibrant festivals, including Chhath Puja, which attracts thousands of devotees annually.
